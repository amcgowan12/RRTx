Rapid Response Privacy Policy

Effective date: August 15, 2025

Rapid Response is designed to work primarily with content stored locally on the device.

Information We Do Not Collect

Rapid Response does not require an account and does not collect personal information for advertising, profiling, or sale.

The app does not currently include third-party analytics, ad trackers, crash-reporting SDKs, account creation, or cloud sync.

Information Stored on Device

Rapid Response may store limited app data locally on your device, including saved favorites and disclaimer acceptance state. This data remains on-device and is not transmitted to us by the app itself.

User-Initiated Email Feedback

If you choose to send feedback by email, the message is sent through Apple Mail or your configured mail app. Your email address and anything you include in that message may be visible to the recipient.

Do not include protected health information or patient identifiers in feedback messages unless your use complies with applicable law and institutional policy.

External Links

The app may open external links such as journal articles, support pages, or reference websites. When you open an external link, that site’s privacy practices apply.

Health and Medical Information

Rapid Response is a clinical reference tool. It is not a medical record and does not replace clinical judgment, pharmacy review, or institutional policy.

Data Sharing

Rapid Response does not share personal data with third parties through in-app tracking or analytics. Information may only be disclosed if required by law or if you choose to share it yourself, such as by sending an email.

Contact

For privacy questions or support requests, use the support information provided on the GitHub repository or App Store listing.
